Artistic Vision

My work exists in the liminal space between painting and sculpture, where two-dimensional surfaces give birth to three-dimensional forms. Each piece is an exploration of the boundary between reality and imagination.

Through the fusion of traditional techniques with contemporary materials, I create works that challenge perception and invite viewers to question the frame that separates art from life.

About the Artist

Elena Cortez is a contemporary visual artist and sculptor whose work challenges the traditional boundaries between painting and sculpture. Born in Barcelona in 1988, she studied at the Royal College of Art in London and currently works between New York and Barcelona.

Her practice is rooted in the exploration of dimensional transformation—how flat surfaces can give birth to three-dimensional forms, and how sculpture can dissolve back into pictorial space. Using a combination of traditional techniques and contemporary materials such as resin, metal, and found objects, she creates works that exist in a state of perpetual becoming.

Elena's work has been exhibited internationally and is held in private collections across Europe and North America. She has received numerous awards including the Turner Prize nomination and the International Sculpture Prize.

Artistic Philosophy

Influences

Drawing inspiration from artists like Anselm Kiefer, Lucio Fontana, and contemporary sculptors pushing the boundaries of mixed media, my work reflects a deep engagement with materiality and the poetics of space.

Process

Each piece begins with an intuitive gesture—a mark, a cut, a protrusion. From there, the work evolves through a dialogue between control and surrender, between the planned and the accidental.
